# CS-250-Software-Development-Lifecycle

# How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?
I interpret user needs by listening to stakeholder requirements and breaking them into smaller, manageable tasks. Creating user stories helps me understand what the user wants to accomplish and why the feature is important. User stories keep the focus on delivering value to the customer instead of simply completing technical tasks. They also make it easier to prioritize work and verify that the finished product meets user expectations.

# How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach software development by breaking large projects into smaller pieces that can be completed and tested incrementally. Throughout this course, I learned that Agile encourages continuous communication, collaboration, and improvement. In future projects, I hope to incorporate Sprint Planning, Daily Scrums, Sprint Reviews, and Sprint Retrospectives because they help teams stay organized, identify problems early, and adapt to changing requirements. I also plan to continue using product backlogs and user stories to organize development work.

# What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member means communicating openly, collaborating with others, and being willing to help solve problems. A successful software developer accepts feedback, shares knowledge with teammates, and takes responsibility for delivering high-quality work. Throughout this course, I learned that every Scrum role contributes to the success of the project. Developers, testers, the Scrum Master, and the Product Owner all work together to achieve a common goal. Strong communication, respect for teammates, and a willingness to continuously improve are essential qualities of an effective software development team member.
